Custom Airframe Manufacturing & Structural Validation

To comply with IMechE UAS Challenge regulation T2.4.5, I transitioned from outsourced COTS sections to a fully in-house composite workflow. This R&D phase focused on replacing standard primary/secondary spars with custom-fabricated structures.

Spar Manufacturing Process

My iterative process moved from wet-layup I-beams to a final circular cross-section spar. Experimentation showed that while I-beams provided excellent bending stiffness, the circular geometry provided the torsional rigidity necessary for medical UAV flight loads.

Carbon Fibre Spar: Structural Results

Load Testing Analysis

Bending Test: The custom spar (mass: 83g) was tested against a predicted root bending moment of 5.5G. Results confirmed the structure withstood these loads without failure, validating our in-house manufacturing quality.

Composite Wings & Material Experimentation

Tested EPS vs XPS foam substitutes for balsa ribs. While EPS was lightweight, its porous nature led to high resin absorption during vacuum bagging. Switching to high-density XPS improved overall strength and surface finish.

To minimize RF interference, carbon fiber was utilized only at the leading edge to improve stagnation point strength and flow attachment, rather than a full-wing wrap.

Vacuum Forming & Electronics Integration

Fuselage & Stack Design

Utilized vacuum forming for the fuselage to achieve a low-drag surface. The electronics stack was designed for rapid reconfiguration, featuring vibration-isolating grommets and a modular mounting rail to allow for fine-tuning the Center of Gravity (CG).
